## DAILY WELLNESS INDICATORS

### **Energy Level (1-5)**

- **4:** Good energy, focused
- **3:** Neutral, steady pace
- **2:** Low energy, struggling
- **1:** Exhausted, needs rest

### **Stress Level (1-5)**
- **1:** No stress, calm
- **2:** Mild stress, manageable
- **3:** Moderate stress, noticeable
- **4:** High stress, affecting work
- **5:** Overwhelmed, needs intervention

### **Work Satisfaction (1-5)**
- **5:** Highly satisfied, engaged
- **4:** Satisfied, motivated
- **3:** Neutral, adequate
- **2:** Dissatisfied, demotivated
- **1:** Very dissatisfied, considering stopping

## WELLNESS INTERVENTION LEVELS

### **Green (All indicators 3+)**
- **Action:** Continue current pace
- **Monitoring:** Weekly check-ins

### **Yellow (Any indicator at 2)**
- **Action:** Check in with agent
- **Monitoring:** Daily follow-up
- **Support:** Offer assistance, adjust workload

### **Red (Any indicator at 1)**
- **Action:** Immediate intervention
- **Monitoring:** Multiple daily check-ins
- **Support:** Reduce workload, provide resources, consider break

---

## BURNOUT PREVENTION STRATEGIES

### **Proactive Measures**
1. **Workload Balance:** Ensure no agent exceeds 25 points/week
2. **Regular Breaks:** Encourage 5-minute breaks every hour
3. **Clear Priorities:** Focus on most important tasks first
4. **Celebrate Wins:** Acknowledge completed work
5. **Open Communication:** Encourage sharing concerns early

### **Reactive Measures**
1. **Workload Redistribution:** Shift tasks from overloaded agents
2. **Temporary Relief:** Give struggling agents lighter tasks
3. **Extended Deadlines:** Adjust timelines if needed
4. **Peer Support:** Pair agents for collaboration
5. **Wellness Day:** Designate a light-work day if needed

---

## WELLNESS RESOURCES

### **For Stress Management**
- Take a 5-minute breathing break
- Step away from screen for 10 minutes
- Prioritize one task at a time
- Ask for help when stuck

### **For Low Energy**
- Review workload balance
- Take a short walk (if possible)
- Break large tasks into smaller steps
- Celebrate small completions

### **For Low Satisfaction**
- Discuss task alignment with interests
- Explore new challenges within project
- Provide constructive feedback
- Recognize contributions
